What ITC is asking for

Five things this idea
has to do at once.

Before the concept, the brief. Every choice ahead is built to answer one of these.

The rut

Everyday meals start to feel repetitive, and the mood on the table drops with them.

Participation

The country should take part, not just watch an ad. All of India, actually in it.

Real tech

The brief asks for genuine AI, working and useful, not a gimmick bolted on top.

Buzz

It needs a hook strong enough to travel on its own and earn real PR conversation.

Scale

It has to carry hundreds of creators without breaking or feeling thin.

The insight

Dal rice again.
Until someone says there is something special.

We eat the same food most days, and it blurs together. Then one special dish lands on the table and the whole mood lifts. ITC MasterChef is that lift. Take it out, cook it in minutes, and an ordinary meal becomes a special one. No skill, no effort, just a warmer table.

Cold fridge in. Warm meal out.
The big idea

The Infinite Recipe Fridge.

One immersive website shaped like a giant fridge that holds the whole country's recipes. This is the answer to participation and buzz in a single object: a shareable centrepiece the whole nation can pour into.

01

You scroll straight through the ITC logo, the camera moving into it. Two lines of text set the mood.

02

A giant fridge appears, the door opens, and you step inside.

03

ITC products sit on the shelves, with real recipes plated around them, shot like food, not a boring list.

04

Every recipe is tagged by where it comes from, from Srirangam to Punjab, so the fridge feels like all of India.

05

Click any recipe and a pop-up plays the video with the steps beside it.

06

Keep scrolling and you move floor by floor, one shelf per state, A to Z, each shelf carrying that region's flavour.

07

A live counter keeps climbing up top, and an Add Yours button lets anyone drop in their own recipe.

Srirangam · TNSeekh Kebab Roll
GujaratHara Bhara Chaat
PunjabPizza Pocket Parcel
HyderabadNugget Biryani Bowl

One page, but it feels like a world. Scroll-driven and animated, a video playing behind the fridge, small humanised characters serving, doors opening. The same craft as the ice cream work, built at fridge scale.

The tech, made real

Genuine AI.
And it stays on our platform.

This is where the brief's tech promise stops being a line and starts working.

Pairing intelligence

Upload a photo of what you are eating and the AI suggests the right MasterChef pairing. Dal rice gets a green kebab, not a pizza pocket. It reads the meal.

Full from day one

From a knowledge base of the products, the AI builds fresh recipe combinations and images, so the fridge feels full before a single creator arrives.

Never empty

We seed it with real starter recipes, then the community keeps adding to it forever.

Built to share

An optional filter and a make-your-own layer, so the fridge spills naturally onto social.

Why on our platform. Every interaction lives on our own site, not on ChatGPT or any outside tool. So ITC keeps the audience, the data, and the branding. People come to the fridge, and everything is wired on the backend.

The hook that makes it spread

Put the fridge on the one thing
this audience touches every day.

A beautiful site is not enough. It needs a reason to travel. So we ride the reach it already has: the Blinkit bag.

Amul, Janmashtami

An empty butter packet that said Krishna finished it before you arrived. Creators turned it into reels on their own, no media push needed.

Coca-Cola, at scale

The same instinct, proven globally: packaging as the invitation. Reference link shared separately.

The move

Print the fridge outline and a QR code on Blinkit delivery bags. Scan for unlimited recipes. The live recipe counter rides along on the pack.

4 in 10

If four in ten of this exact audience order from Blinkit almost daily, a huge share is reached before they ever open the site. Every parcel becomes the ad, whether or not they scan.

Har Roz Kuch Special,
built by all of India.

The fridge is open. The counter is already moving. Now we fill it together.
